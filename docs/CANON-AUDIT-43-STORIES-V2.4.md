# CariDream 43-Story Canon Audit

**Canon standard:** CariDream World Bible Canon Edition v2.4  
**Audit date:** June 11, 2026  
**Collection audited:** Firestore `stories`  
**Scope:** 43 complete story texts across 7 countries and 10 current series  
**Changes made to stories:** None

## Executive Finding

The collection contains **no direct contradiction of a Permanent Pillar** and no story attempts to replace Manman Mirwa, the Moonlight Wanderer, or the Moon Conch.

The collection is broadly compatible with Canon Edition v2.4, but it is not yet uniformly canon-polished.

The main issues are:

1. Several endings explain their lesson directly instead of allowing the listener to discover it.
2. A few lessons and symbols repeat across islands without enough island-specific distinction.
3. Some current series names do not match the locations or narrative identity of all episodes assigned to them.
4. Some ordinary natural features are called "guardians," which could blur the formal Guardian tier.
5. A few culturally or historically grounded stories need source review before being treated as definitive representations.

### Role and Hierarchy Ambiguities

#### Ordinary "guardians"

Several stories use guardian language for mountains, cacti, trees, or animals:

- Cactus Shadows at Dusk
- The Sleepy Divi-Divi Tree
- Maya's Coral Reef Lullaby
- Shadows of the Twin Pitons

This is poetic rather than contradictory, but Canon Edition v2.4 uses **Guardian** as a formal hierarchy tier.

**Recommendation:** Reserve capitalized `Guardian` for canonical Guardians. Use words such as `watchful`, `sheltering`, `protective`, or `steady presence` for ordinary natural features.

#### The Sea Turtle identity

The World Bible establishes **The Sea Turtle** as Keeper of Ocean Stories. The collection currently presents:

- Luma in The Turtle Beneath the Stars
- Maya in Maya's Coral Reef Lullaby

Neither is clearly identified as the canonical Sea Turtle.

**Recommendation:** Designate Maya as the recurring Sea Turtle guardian, or explicitly state that both Maya and Luma are ordinary turtles whose stories are remembered by the canonical Sea Turtle. Do not leave two implied guardians with the same role.

#### Divi-Divi identity

The World Bible establishes the **Divi-Divi Spirits** as Aruban Guardians of Travelers. Existing stories feature a physical Divi-Divi tree but do not clearly distinguish the tree from the Spirits.

**Recommendation:** Treat the tree as a place where the Divi-Divi Spirits may be felt or heard, not automatically as the full guardian collective.

## Cultural and Historical Review

### Haiti: Citadel Dreams

The first four Haiti stories respectfully emphasize freedom, memory, courage, family, and remembrance. They do not contradict canon.

However, they simplify the Citadelle into generalized peaceful strength. UNESCO describes the Citadelle, Sans-Souci, and Ramiers as early post-independence monuments and universal symbols of liberty built by formerly enslaved Black people who had won freedom. Future revisions should preserve that specific historical meaning rather than relying only on vague stone-and-mountain symbolism.

**Recommendation:** Retain the sleep-friendly tone while adding reviewed details about Haitian independence, Henri Christophe's period, the northern location, architecture, community memory, and the site's complex history.

### CariDream Interpretations

The current 43 stories do not yet use La Diablesse, Cigua Spirits, Silver Ribbon Spirits, Manman Mirwa, the Old Walker, Gatekeeper, Felix, or Zim.

That absence is not a problem. Future stories using these figures must preserve the cultural-care labels and role boundaries established in v2.4.

### Island-Specific Nature

The collection often uses moonlight, stars, fireflies, rivers, bamboo, and mist. These are valid shared Caribbean experiences, but the Island Rule requires the **meaning and cultural context** to remain distinct.

## Duplicated Lessons

### Small Light Reveals the Way

Strong overlap:

- The Turtle Beneath the Stars
- The Firefly Lantern
- Papa Bois and the Firefly Path
- Lanterns of Cap-Haitien

The first three all use a small light to guide the next part of a journey.

**Recommendation:**

- Bahamas: emphasize navigation, currents, stars, and ocean belonging.
- Jamaica: emphasize communal light, storytelling, and Anansi keeping a promise.
- Trinidad & Tobago: emphasize forest trust and the agouti's relationship with Papa Bois.
- Haiti: emphasize remembrance and intergenerational continuity rather than physical guidance.

### Letting Worries Drift Away

Strong overlap:

- Palm Beach After Midnight
- The River That Hummed
- Rain Over the Southern Hills
- Papa Bois and the Whispering Silk Cotton
- Papa Bois and the Moonlit River

**Recommendation:** Give each island a different emotional action:

- Aruba: trade winds carry away the busy day.
- Dominican Republic: water transforms feeling into rhythm.
- Trinidad & Tobago: forest beings release what they have carried for others.

Papa Bois and the Moonlit River is closest to the Dominican river motif and needs the strongest differentiation.

### Rest Is Strength

Strong overlap:

- The Lullaby of the Doctor Bird
- The Sleepy Blue Mountains
- Papa Bois and the Sleeping Bamboo
- Moon Over Blue Mountain Peak
- The Turtle Beneath the Stars
- The Soft Road to Samana

**Recommendation:** Preserve the Papa Bois story as the canonical Sleeping Bamboo legend. Reframe Doctor Bird stories around hummingbird rhythm and Jamaican mountain identity rather than repeating the same explicit message.

### Shared Light and Shared Joy

Strong overlap:

- The Moonlight Mango
- Anansi and the Moonlit Tricks
- The Firefly Lantern

All three conclude that light, magic, or joy becomes better when shared.

**Recommendation:** Keep the shared theme within Anansi Adventures, but vary the character movement:

- Mango: generosity
- Moonlit Tricks: wonder cannot be owned
- Firefly Lantern: promises become communal acts

### Memory as a Guiding Light

Related but culturally distinguishable:

- The Lighthouse Says Goodnight
- Lanterns of Cap-Haitien
- The Lantern of Castries Bay
- Drums Beneath the Stars

**Recommendation:** Keep all four, but protect their distinct identities:

- Aruba: family across distance
- Haiti: national and generational remembrance
- Saint Lucia: home and family memory
- Haiti drums: embodied communal continuity

## Duplicated Island Identities

### Jamaica: Blue Mountains and Doctor Bird

Highest duplication risk in the collection:

- Moon Over Blue Mountain Peak
- The Lullaby of the Doctor Bird
- The Blue Mountain Moonlight Mist
- The Sleepy Blue Mountains

Three stories feature the Doctor Bird, mist, moonlight, and mountain rest. The Sleepy Blue Mountains is especially close to The Lullaby of the Doctor Bird and The Blue Mountain Moonlight Mist.

**Recommendation:** Consolidate these into a deliberate Doctor Bird Island Legend thread. Give each episode a distinct purpose, or retire one narrative after preserving its strongest passages.

### Haiti: One Series, Two Identities

`Citadel Dreams` currently contains:

- Four northern history and remembrance stories
- Two Jacmel firefly stories from the southern coast

The Jacmel stories are culturally and geographically distinct from the Citadel.

**Recommendation:** Keep Episodes 1-4 in `Citadel Dreams`. Move Episodes 5-6 into a separate Jacmel series, such as `Golden Nights of Jacmel`.

### Dominican Republic: Series Name Too Narrow

`River Lullabies of Quisqueya` includes rivers, roads, rain, Jarabacoa waterfalls, Constanza stars, crickets, and mountain mist.

The stories form a coherent Dominican nature collection, but not every episode is a river lullaby.

**Recommendation:** Either broaden the series name or create location threads:

- River Lullabies
- Jarabacoa Mountain Dreams
- Constanza Valley Nights

### Bahamas: Ocean Series Split

The Turtle Beneath the Stars sits under generic `Bedtime Stories`, while the other turtle story is in `Ocean Dreams of the Bahamas`.

**Recommendation:** Move The Turtle Beneath the Stars into the Bahamian ocean grouping and then evaluate both turtle stories for the Major Series `Whispers of Stars Beneath the Sea`.

### Saint Lucia: Two Natural Identities in One Series

`Piton Moon Dreams` contains:

- Piton and Soufrière Bay stories
- Rainforest and bamboo stories

Both are Saint Lucian, but they represent distinct environments.

**Recommendation:** Preserve `Piton Moon Dreams` for the Pitons and bay. Develop Liam's rainforest stories as a separate local thread if more episodes are added.

### Aruba: Strongest Island Coherence

`Trade Wind Lullabies` consistently uses the trade winds, desert, Divi-Divi trees, Shoco, beaches, and lighthouse.

**Finding:** This is the clearest execution of the Island Rule in the current collection.

## Recommended Canon Priorities

### Priority 1: Resolve Before Declaring the Entire Library Canon-Polished

1. Clarify the canonical Sea Turtle identity.
2. Differentiate The Firefly Lantern from Papa Bois and the Firefly Path.
3. Resolve the duplicate Doctor Bird/Blue Mountain stories.
4. Split Jacmel stories from Citadel Dreams.
5. Soften direct lesson statements in the Papa Bois and Anansi endings.

### Priority 2: Strengthen the Island Rule

1. Broaden or split River Lullabies of Quisqueya.
2. Separate Piton and rainforest threads if Saint Lucia expands.
3. Give shared motifs different cultural meanings on each island.
4. Reserve formal Guardian language for canonical Guardians.

### Priority 3: Expansion Opportunities

1. Establish the Sea Turtle Major Series.
2. Develop Divi-Divi Spirits through Aruban travel and direction stories.
3. Build the Doctor Bird as a Jamaican Island Legend.
4. Develop Jacmel's fireflies as an art-and-wonder series.
5. Introduce Whispers Beneath the Moonlight through careful Wanderer framing.

## Final Assessment

- **Permanent Pillar continuity:** Pass
- **Character hierarchy:** Pass with Sea Turtle and guardian-wording clarifications
- **Island Rule:** Partial pass; strong locations but repeated lessons need differentiation
- **Never Teach the Lesson:** Revision needed in multiple stories
- **Wonder Over Fear:** Pass
- **Sleep First:** Pass
- **Caribbean First:** Pass with targeted historical and cultural review
- **Series architecture:** Functional but needs reorganization

The collection is a strong beta library. Its primary need is not more lore; it is sharper differentiation, quieter lessons, and cleaner series architecture.
